# CONTENT-004 — Visitor Journey Constitution Review

**Mission:** OPERATION PHOENIX / CONTENT-004. **Type:** Experience Architecture Review — the entire website evaluated as one visitor journey, not as individual pages. Recommendations only; no content rewritten, no pages redesigned.
**Reviewed against:** `PDC-001` Article VIII (Homepage Narrative Constitution) and Articles III/IV/VII, `content/CONTENT-001-Services/` (`CONTENT-001` through `CONTENT-003`), `CRN-001`, plus the **live site**, checked directly rather than assumed — current WordPress navigation menu (menu ID 4, "Top Menu Primary") and current published page list, retrieved live for this review.
**Review perspective:** a first-time executive visitor — Managing Director, Operations Director, Manufacturing Executive, Investment Committee member, or Public Sector Decision Maker — asking "what journey do I experience?"

---

## A methodological correction, made before the review starts

The mission brief's own evaluation sequence (§4 "Transition to Leadership," then §5 "Transition to Case Studies") assumes Leadership precedes Case Studies. **The live site's actual order, confirmed directly against the current navigation menu and the current homepage section sequence, is Case Studies before Leadership** — on both the primary nav (`Services → Industries → Case Studies → Leadership → Testimonials`) and the homepage's own single-page section order. This review evaluates the real order, not the assumed one. Getting this backwards would have produced a review of a journey the visitor doesn't actually take — worth stating plainly rather than silently substituting.

## The single most important finding, stated up front

**The live Services page (970) does not yet reflect the constitutionally-approved 9-service catalogue.** `CMS-005` (the deployment package that would put the new 9-service catalogue live) remains undeployed as of this review — confirmed directly, not assumed, from this engagement's own record. The content a visitor actually sees today on `/services/` is the pre-`CONTENT-001` catalogue (an 8-card structure: Energy studies, Efficiency assessments, Decarbonisation, Grant funding, Procurement support, Project delivery, Commissioning, Performance verification, plus R&D Support and Technology Validation), pushed live on 2026-07-05 — before the 9-service catalogue, `CONTENT-002`'s boundary review, `CONTENT-002A`'s Stage × Domain model, or `CONTENT-003`'s publication-ready copy existed. **Every finding in this review about the 9-service portfolio (§3, Services Narrative) describes the approved-but-unpublished architecture, not the visitor's current live experience**, and the two are labelled separately throughout.

## Journey Map

### Path 1 — Homepage-first visitor (arrives at `/`, reads top to bottom)

Confirmed current section order, live: **Hero (Executive Trust) → Industries (Who We Help) → Problems (Commercial Challenge) → Solutions (Why IEP) → Funding Capability → Process (Methodology) → Services → Case Studies → Leadership → Technology (Technical Capability) → Testimonials → Final CTA.**

Compared against `PDC-001` Article VIII's governing sequence — **Executive Trust → Commercial Challenge → Who We Help → Why IEP → Services → Methodology → Technical Capability → Leadership → Call To Action** — three deviations exist:

1. **Who We Help and Commercial Challenge are swapped.** The Constitution puts the business problem (stage 2) before audience definition (stage 3); the live page puts audience definition first.
2. **Technical Capability has moved from before Leadership to after it** (and after Case Studies, which the Constitution doesn't model at all). Article VIII's own stated Transition Logic for Technical Capability is "Who will I work with?" — explicitly designed as the question that leads into Leadership. Moved to after Leadership, it no longer performs that function.
3. **Three sections exist on the live page with no stage in Article VIII's model:** Funding Capability, Case Studies, Testimonials. All three are additive, not contradictory, but the Constitution as written does not account for them.

### Path 2 — Multi-page visitor (uses primary navigation)

Confirmed live, from the site's actual navigation menu: **Home → About → Services → Industries → Case Studies → Leadership → Testimonials → Insights → Contact.**

The homepage is deliberately excerpt-only by design (a documented content principle: "the homepage stays excerpt-only — full detail routes to internal pages") — so this path is not redundant with Path 1, it's the depth layer beneath it. A visitor who scrolls the homepage and then clicks into, say, Case Studies, is deliberately getting more of what they already saw a preview of.

### Path 3 — Direct-entry visitor (arrives via search or a shared link, bypassing the homepage)

Not explicitly asked for in the mission brief, but material to "what journey does this visitor experience" — a real and common entry pattern. Each internal page carries its own hero/content/CTA structure, so a direct-entry visitor isn't dropped into a bare content fragment. But they do miss the specific, deliberately-sequenced Executive Trust → Commercial Challenge → Why IEP arc the Constitution built for the homepage — there is no equivalent structured trust-building sequence reproduced on, for example, the Case Studies or Services pages themselves. This is a real gap in the *journey*, distinct from a gap in any single page's content.

## Strengths

1. **Executive Trust leads on both paths.** Every entry point — homepage hero, or any internal page's own hero — opens with senior-led, credible positioning before capability detail, consistent with `PDC-001` Article IV and Article VII's messaging hierarchy (Business Outcome before Technical Detail).
2. **Case Studies placed directly after Services, before Leadership, on both the homepage and the nav.** This is strong, evidence-first sequencing — a visitor sees a capability claim, then immediate proof, before being asked to trust the people behind it. This is *not* modelled in Article VIII at all, but it's a good addition, not a defect (see Priority Recommendations).
3. **Testimonials placed immediately before the Final CTA on the homepage.** Trust reinforcement at the exact point a visitor is about to be asked to act — good commercial-journey logic (Trust → Action), and consistent with the general Problem → Solution → Evidence → Trust → Action model even though Article VIII doesn't name this stage.
4. **The Methodology section is deliberately light-touch, by explicit client instruction** ("a restaurant doesn't explain how they cook the food... sell the finished product"), preserved correctly in the live copy. This is not a density gap — it's a documented, intentional decision, and this review does not recommend expanding it.
5. **The service portfolio's underlying architecture (once deployed) is genuinely coherent as one consulting narrative**, not nine independent offerings — `CONTENT-002A`'s Stage × Domain model gives Screening → domain engineering → Feasibility → Funding/Delivery a real lifecycle logic, with Technology Innovation positioned honestly as a parallel track rather than forced into the sequence. See §3 below.

## Weaknesses

1. **The Services page a visitor actually sees today does not reflect any of the `CONTENT-001`–`CONTENT-003` work.** This is the review's headline finding — see above. Everything below in §3 describes an approved architecture, not a live experience.
2. **Insights (983) is a published, linked, navigable page with no real content** ("scaffold, no real articles yet"). A visitor curious enough to click a primary nav item and find it thin is a worse outcome than the item not being in primary navigation at all.
3. **A visitor landing directly on an internal page misses the homepage's deliberately sequenced trust arc** (Path 3 above) — no equivalent structured narrative exists elsewhere on the site.
4. **Two orphaned, real content pages exist outside any navigation:** Quality Management (669) and Grants (648). A visitor journey review can't map a path to content the site itself doesn't route anyone toward — these pages are currently invisible to every one of the three paths above.
5. **Careers (982) and Resources (984) are live, published scaffolds** ("no real roles yet" / "no real assets yet") not present in primary navigation — lower risk than Insights since they're less likely to be stumbled into, but still live URLs a search engine could surface to a visitor expecting real content.

## Narrative Breaks

1. **The Technical Capability → Leadership transition Article VIII designed no longer exists on the live page**, because Technical Capability now sits after Leadership instead of before it (see Journey Map, Path 1, deviation 2). This is the clearest single narrative break in the review: a constitutionally-designed transition ("Who will I work with?" → meet the team) has been structurally undone by reordering, even though neither section's own content was rewritten.
2. **Who We Help ahead of Commercial Challenge is a milder break.** It still functions — a visitor can reasonably read "here are the sectors we serve" before "here's the problem you have" — but it inverts Article VIII's own stated Visitor Question sequence (stage 2's "Is this relevant to my business?" was designed to precede stage 3's "Is this company built for organisations like mine?").
3. **The soft CTA at the end of the Methodology section** ("See how this works on your site → Book Opportunity Screening") asks for action before the visitor has seen the Services section that follows it. Minor, but it's a CTA appearing ahead of the capability detail it should logically follow — see Calls-to-Action, below.

## Redundancies

1. **None found within the homepage-to-internal-page relationship itself** — the excerpt/full-detail split is deliberate and, on the evidence reviewed, correctly maintained (homepage sections stay short; internal pages carry the depth).
2. **Two structurally near-duplicate offerings inside the approved-but-unpublished service architecture**, both already resolved by `CONTENT-002A` and worth restating here in visitor-journey terms rather than architecture terms: a visitor reading Services 2 and 5 side by side, absent `CONTENT-002A`'s Rule B framing, could reasonably ask why "fuel switching" appears under two different services. `CONTENT-003`'s enhanced copy already states each service's driver-based scope explicitly, which should prevent this from surfacing once deployed — flagged here only to confirm the fix is genuinely visitor-facing, not just an internal classification exercise.
3. **Legacy Envato demo pages** (Homepage 1–8, About Us 1–6, Project Style 1/2, Blog Classic/Grid View, Shop/Cart/Checkout/My Account, Services – Old, Home – Old, Contact – Old) remain published and, in principle, reachable by direct URL or a stale external link/search index entry. None are in any navigation a real visitor would follow, so they don't affect the mapped journeys above, but they are a latent redundancy risk (a search engine indexing "Homepage 3," last modified 2026-06-30, is a plausible bad outcome) — noted for completeness, not a priority for this review's purpose.

## Transition Improvements

*(Recommendations only, per the mission's Repository Rules — no content or layout has been changed.)*

1. Reconcile `PDC-001` Article VIII with the live homepage's actual, evidently deliberate section order — either amend the Constitution to formally recognise Funding Capability, Case Studies, and Testimonials as stages (they earn their place, per Strengths above), and to record the Who-We-Help/Commercial-Challenge swap as an approved change, or restore the page to match the Constitution. Leaving the two silently divergent, as they are now, means neither document is trustworthy as a description of the other.
2. Specifically re-examine Technical Capability's position. Article VIII's own transition logic for that stage only makes sense positioned before Leadership. Either move the section back, or formally re-author its transition logic for its new position — right now it has neither.
3. Consider whether the Methodology section's closing soft CTA should move to after the Services section instead, so the CTA follows the capability claim it references rather than preceding it.
4. If the 9-service catalogue is deployed to the live Services page in future, verify at that point that the homepage's own Services section (currently an excerpt of the *old* catalogue) is updated in step — a homepage/internal-page mismatch on the service list itself would be a much more visible break than anything in this review.

## Priority Recommendations

Ranked by what most affects whether a visitor experiences one coherent narrative versus disconnected pages.

1. **Highest — deploy or explicitly schedule `CMS-005`.** Every architectural improvement made in `CONTENT-001` through `CONTENT-003` is currently invisible to every real visitor. This review's other findings about the 9-service portfolio are preparation, not yet outcome.
2. **High — resolve the Technical Capability / Leadership transition break.** This is the one clear case where a section move has actively undone a documented piece of narrative design, not just left a gap.
3. **Medium — reconcile Article VIII with the live homepage order**, formally, so the Constitution describes the site that exists rather than a slightly different one.
4. **Medium — address Insights.** A thin, empty-feeling page sitting in primary navigation is a worse visitor outcome than no page at all; either populate it or reconsider its nav placement until it's ready.
5. **Low — decide the fate of Quality Management, Grants, Careers, and Resources.** None of these currently affect a visitor's mapped journey (they're outside every nav), but each is either real content going to waste (Quality Management, Grants) or a scaffold that shouldn't be publicly reachable yet (Careers, Resources) — worth a deliberate decision rather than remaining in this undecided, orphaned state indefinitely.
