## Executive Summary

Energy, Utilities & Process Efficiency covers the engineering work of reducing energy, utilities and process waste at an existing industrial or commercial site — audits and monitoring, optimisation of steam, refrigeration, compressed air and CO₂ systems, and manufacturing-process improvements, most concretely delivered through waste-heat recovery engineering.

Clients engage IEP here once a specific opportunity has been identified and funded, typically following Opportunity Screening and an Engineering Design, Feasibility & Investment Case study. The work itself is hands-on: analysing exhaust and flue-gas conditions, designing heat exchangers and recovery systems, and integrating them into existing production and building-services infrastructure, including cases where earlier in-house or third-party recovery attempts had already failed.

The expected outcome is a measured reduction in energy or fuel cost, verified against the original consumption baseline. Three delivered engagements evidence this directly, with realised annual savings ranging from the tens of thousands of pounds to over £150,000, alongside measurable reductions in fuel consumption and CO₂ emissions.

## Business Challenges

- Energy and utilities costs that directly erode margin in energy-intensive operations.
- Waste heat routinely vented to atmosphere rather than recovered, in some cases after earlier recovery attempts failed due to difficult exhaust conditions.
- Process configurations — cyclical or asymmetrical operating patterns, for example — that make off-the-shelf efficiency solutions genuinely inapplicable.
- Large fuel volumes consumed in ancillary processes, with the resulting heat simply discharged rather than reused.

## Capabilities

**Energy & Utilities Management**
- Energy audits, monitoring and reporting
- Energy reduction strategies
- Utilities optimisation across steam, refrigeration, compressed air and CO₂ systems
- Fuel switching, where the primary driver is cost or process efficiency

**Process Efficiency**
- Process optimisation and manufacturing-efficiency improvement, at the point of delivery
- Operational-excellence improvements to existing production lines

**Waste Heat Recovery Engineering**
- Flue-gas and exhaust-condition analysis
- Heat exchanger design and integration
- Recovery technology for high-temperature, corrosive-exhaust conditions
- Thermal-battery-based recovery for cyclical or asymmetrical processes

## Typical Deliverables

- Installed heat recovery systems (heat exchangers, thermal batteries)
- Quantified consumption and cost-reduction outcomes, measured against the original baseline
- Improved operational flexibility as a delivered, secondary outcome

## Business Outcomes

- ≈£60,000 annual saving, ~48-month payback — heat recovery from RTO exhaust, plastic packaging manufacturer
- ≈£150,000 annual natural gas saving — waste heat recovery, aluminium recycling plant, following previously failed recovery attempts
- £84,000 annual fuel cost reduction, plus improved operational flexibility — thermal battery heat recovery, specialist brick manufacturer
- Up to 25% reduction in energy consumption and 10–40% reduction in fuel consumption, reported across the wider portfolio of engagements
