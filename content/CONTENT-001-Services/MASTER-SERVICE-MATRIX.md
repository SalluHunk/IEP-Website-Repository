## Cross-cutting boundary questions (appear in more than one service file)

These aren't per-service gaps so much as places where the same evidence could reasonably attach to two different services in the new 9-service model. Recorded once here to avoid the project team re-discovering them independently per file:

- **"Energy studies" / "Efficiency assessments"** — could belong to Service 1 (diagnostic) or Service 6 (feasibility study). Flagged in both files.
- **Grant funding for R&D/innovation work (Innovate UK, Horizon Europe, Dr Asthana's £3.1M/£9.5M figures)** — could belong to Service 7 (general funding capability) or Service 9 (innovation-specific funding). Flagged in both files.
- **"Investment Approved" case outcomes (Cases 2, 3, 4)** — implicitly involved Service 6-type feasibility/investment-case work, but that work is only described where it's attributed to the delivering service (2, 4, 5), not independently. Whether Service 6 should reference these cases directly, or leave them solely attributed to their delivering service, is an open convention question, not a factual gap.

None of these need new evidence to resolve — they need a project-team or client decision about which service "owns" evidence that genuinely supports more than one title.
