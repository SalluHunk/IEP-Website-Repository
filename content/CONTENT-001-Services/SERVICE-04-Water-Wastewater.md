## Executive Summary

Water, Wastewater & Circular Resource Management covers engineering work that reduces water and wastewater treatment costs and recovers value from process by-product streams that would otherwise be treated as pure waste. The anchor evidence is a full utilities survey and containerised anaerobic digestion installation for a brewery client facing a sharp, externally-driven rise in effluent charges, alongside a separate protein- and biomass-recovery project converting spent grain into a revenue-generating output.

Clients typically engage IEP here when facing rising effluent or wastewater charges — often the result of a municipal or regulatory change outside their control — or when an existing waste stream could plausibly be recovered as a resource rather than simply disposed of.

The expected outcome is a controlled, quantified reduction in water or wastewater cost exposure, and, where a resource-recovery opportunity exists, a new or reduced-cost input stream. This work draws on genuine water and wastewater engineering expertise held at director level, including formal qualification in Civil Engineering with Water Resource Management.

## Business Challenges

- Effluent or wastewater treatment charges rising for reasons outside the client's direct control, such as a municipal charging change.
- Process by-products currently sent to low-value disposal routes, representing an unrecovered resource rather than a pure cost.
- Regulatory exposure tied to discharge quality — COD-based charging, for example — that is difficult to manage without a dedicated utilities survey.

## Capabilities

**Water & Wastewater Engineering**
- Full utilities surveys
- Anaerobic digestion (AD) plant specification, design and installation
- Biogas recovery and on-site treatment, including blending with natural gas for boiler use

**Circular & Resource Recovery**
- Protein recovery from process by-product streams
- Biomass fuel production from recovered material
- Zero-waste operation design

## Typical Deliverables

- A specified, designed and installed containerised anaerobic digestion plant
- An integrated biogas recovery and treatment system, combined with existing boiler or steam infrastructure
- A protein recovery and biomass fuel production process, where a resource-recovery opportunity exists

## Business Outcomes

- Effluent treatment cost impact reduced from an estimated ~€750,000/year increase to under €100,000/year — urban brewery wastewater project
- Natural gas consumption reduced by 8–10% through on-site biogas substitution
- 35% of a spent-grain waste stream recovered as a high-value protein ingredient, with a 75% reduction in associated natural gas demand and a ~80% carbon reduction — commercial figure not yet available for this case, see Evidence Integrity Check
