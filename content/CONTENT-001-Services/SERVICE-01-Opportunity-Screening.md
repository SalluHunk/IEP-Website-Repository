## Executive Summary

Opportunity Screening is IEP's entry point: a focused, 30–45 minute diagnostic review that identifies where energy, utilities, water and process waste are likely being lost across a client's site. It is not a full audit. It draws on the same site-survey, utilities-assessment and gap-analysis discipline IEP applies throughout its wider engagements, scoped to produce a fast, practical read on where the opportunity sits.

Clients typically engage IEP at this stage when they suspect inefficiency exists — unexplained energy bills, rising utilities costs, a project idea that has stalled — but lack the internal capacity or objectivity to isolate and prioritise it. The screening closes that gap.

The expected outcome is not a finished business case; it is clarity. A client leaves knowing which savings areas are worth pursuing, what a pragmatic next step looks like, and whether funding support is likely to be available, informing the decision to proceed to a full feasibility study.

## Business Challenges

- Energy or utilities costs that don't track with production output, with no clear internal explanation.
- Efficiency improvements identified informally but never prioritised or actioned.
- Limited in-house capacity or objectivity to systematically locate and rank opportunities across a site.
- Uncertainty about whether a suspected inefficiency is significant enough to justify a deeper study.

## Capabilities

**Site & Data Diagnostics**
- Site surveys and utilities assessments
- Gap analysis and process mapping
- Instrumentation and data-gathering upgrades, where existing data is insufficient
- Data collection and validation

**Opportunity Identification**
- Pinpointing where waste and inefficiency can be practically addressed
- Broad-level energy consumption analysis across sites and processes, to establish a baseline before deeper study begins

## Typical Deliverables

- A structured read-out of likely savings areas
- An outlined, pragmatic next step
- An initial indication of whether funding support is likely to be relevant

*No formal named document (a "screening report," for example) is confirmed in the evidence — the deliverable above describes what a client receives, not a fixed document format. See Evidence Integrity Check.*

## Business Outcomes

- A defined shortlist of savings opportunities, ready to take into a feasibility study
- A faster, better-informed decision on whether to proceed to Service 6 (Engineering Design, Feasibility & Investment Case)

*No financial outcome figures apply specifically to the screening stage itself — the measurable financial outcomes evidenced across the portfolio (Service 2, Service 4) are delivered later, once a screened opportunity has gone through Feasibility and Delivery.*
