## Executive Summary

Low-carbon & Resilient Energy Systems covers engineering work that reduces the carbon intensity of a client's energy supply: renewable integration, fuel switching driven by decarbonisation rather than cost alone, hydrogen adoption, solar thermal generation, thermal storage and electrification strategy. The clearest evidence is a new-build brewery design achieving 100% of thermal demand from renewable and recovered sources, combining solar thermal collectors, an 800m³ thermal storage system and waste heat recovery.

Clients typically engage IEP here when reducing carbon exposure is a stated objective in its own right — a new facility designed from the outset for low-carbon operation, for example — rather than as a secondary benefit of an efficiency project.

The expected outcome is a quantified reduction in carbon intensity, evidenced by delivered renewable and recovered-energy supply. **The "resilient" component of this service is not yet defined by any available evidence source** and should not be described in published content until the client confirms what it refers to — grid resilience, supply security, or another meaning.

## Business Challenges

- Exposure to energy cost volatility tied to fossil-fuel-dependent supply.
- Regulatory and carbon-cost exposure that will increase as decarbonisation requirements tighten.
- New facilities that must be designed for low-carbon operation from the outset, rather than retrofitted later at greater cost.
