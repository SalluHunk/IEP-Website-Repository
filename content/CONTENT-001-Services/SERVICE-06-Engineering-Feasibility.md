## Executive Summary

Engineering Design, Feasibility & Investment Case converts a diagnosed opportunity into an investment-grade business case: financial modelling, investment appraisal, risk assessment, and the engineering design and specification needed to justify a capital decision. IEP's own methodology explicitly prioritises financial viability before committing to detailed engineering cost — feasibility studies here are built specifically for capital-investment approval, not as a general-purpose report.

Clients engage IEP once an opportunity has been identified, typically through Opportunity Screening, and needs converting into something a Board can approve — a case robust enough to withstand scrutiny from both Finance and Engineering functions at once.

The expected outcome is a decision-ready proposal: a specified engineering solution, supported by mass and energy balances, cost analysis, and investment appraisal, that a client's internal or Board approval process can act on directly. This capability sits behind every "Investment Approved" outcome across IEP's delivered engagements, whichever technical domain the project ultimately belongs to.

## Business Challenges

- Capital investment approval that requires evidence-grade financial justification, not an engineering recommendation alone.
- A recognised gap between identifying an opportunity and converting it into a delivered, funded project.
- Recommendations that must satisfy both Finance and Engineering functions simultaneously, a bar most single-discipline advisers cannot meet.
