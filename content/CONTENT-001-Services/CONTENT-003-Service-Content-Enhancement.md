# CONTENT-003 — Service Content Enhancement

**Mission:** OPERATION PHOENIX / CONTENT-003. **Type:** Content Materialization — transforms the constitutionally approved service documents into publication-ready website content. Does not alter the approved business architecture (`CONTENT-002A`, frozen per ABR-001).
**Scope:** all 9 `SERVICE-0X-*.md` files updated in place. This document records what changed, why, and how ready each service now is.

## What changed, portfolio-wide

Every `SERVICE-0X-*.md` file was restructured from `CONTENT-001`'s evidence-audit format (Executive Summary / Business Challenges / Capabilities / Typical Deliverables / Industries / Supporting Evidence table / per-section Confidence Rating / Outstanding Questions) into a publication-ready format: **Executive Summary (120–180 words) / Business Challenges / Capabilities / Typical Deliverables / Business Outcomes (new) / Evidence Integrity Check (condensed)**. The original per-statement citation tables and full Outstanding Questions lists are not reproduced in each file — they remain intact and authoritative in `CHANGELOG.md`'s 2026-07-13 entry and `MASTER-SERVICE-MATRIX.md`, so no traceability is lost, only de-duplicated from the publication-facing copy.

**Business Outcomes** is a new section, not present in `CONTENT-001`. It exists specifically to surface the case-study figures and delivered results that were previously buried inside "Supporting Evidence" tables, so a reader can see proof of outcome without reading a citation table.

**No case studies, statistics, client names, or technology claims were invented.** Every figure in every Business Outcomes section already existed in `CONTENT-001`. Where a section had a Gap rating in `CONTENT-001`, it retains that Gap rating here (reworded for readability, not filled).

## Cross-Service Review

Conducted after all 9 files were enhanced, per the mission's required pass.

- **Terminology:** Applied `CONTENT-002A` Rule E (decarbonisation/low-carbon/net-zero/carbon-reduction hierarchy) consistently — "decarbonisation" used as the umbrella outcome term, "low-carbon energy systems" reserved for Service 5's domain, "net-zero" reserved for the one stated 100% outcome (Service 5's Case 6), "carbon reduction" used only in results/statistics contexts. No further drift found across the 9 files.
- **Tone:** All 9 files now open their Executive Summary with what the service is, follow with when a client engages IEP, and close with the expected outcome — a consistent three-beat structure, deliberately varied in sentence construction file-to-file so no two summaries read as templated (per the Writing Principles' instruction to avoid repeated sentence structures).
- **Section order:** Identical across all 9 files (Executive Summary → Business Challenges → Capabilities → Typical Deliverables → Business Outcomes → Evidence Integrity Check), including Services 3 and 8, which populate the same headings with explicit "Not applicable" / "Gap" content rather than a different structure.
- **Level of detail:** Deliberately uneven, and left that way — Services 2, 6, and 7 carry the most detail because they have the most evidence; Services 3 and 8 carry the least, for the same reason. Forcing equal length across all 9 would have meant padding the weaker files with unsupported material, which the Repository Rules prohibit.
- **Duplicated wording removed:** The clearest instance was Service 6's Business Outcomes section, which in an earlier draft repeated the Service 2/4/5 case-study figures verbatim. Rewritten to state Service 6's own contribution (the investment-case work underlying those outcomes) rather than re-quoting the figures a second time. Fuel-switching and renewable-integration language was similarly checked across Services 2 and 5 to confirm each now states its own driver-based scope (Rule B) rather than both listing the identical unscoped capability.
