## Approved Design Decisions

Only approved decisions are recorded in this section.

### Colour Philosophy
- Preserve existing IEP branding.
- Maintain a predominantly light visual design.
- White backgrounds remain primary.
- Use graphite only as accent sections.
- Green remains the primary accent colour.
- Avoid dramatic dark UI.
- Avoid futuristic/cyber styling.

This supersedes the Statement of Work's original Teal/Coral working palette (§2.6, Branding & Visual Identity), which is recorded there as a starting concept-development direction, not a final specification. The Client Questionnaire's Design Validation section (§10) independently corroborates the green-accent, light-background direction.

### Typography
- Increase heading sizes significantly.
- Strong visual hierarchy.
- Executive appearance.
- Generous whitespace.
- Typography should create visual impact rather than decoration.

### Homepage Narrative

The approved homepage story sequence is:

Executive Trust → Commercial Challenge → Who We Help → Why IEP → Services → Methodology → Technical Capability → Leadership → Call To Action

This sequence supersedes any different homepage ordering wherever one exists. No distinct "original" homepage-ordering document (Approved Sitemap or Homepage Wireframe) has been located in the source archive to compare against — both remain catalogued as Missing in `KNOWLEDGE-SOURCES-001` — so this sequence is recorded here as the governing order going forward.

### Imagery
- Authentic industrial facilities.
- Engineering environments.
- Energy-intensive industries.
- Real implementation.
- Avoid generic corporate stock photography.
- Avoid decorative technology imagery.
- Diagrams may be used where they improve understanding.

Corroborated by the Website Designer Instructions' Industrial Image Library Brief (energy systems, process engineering, utilities, resource recovery, engineering visualisation; explicitly avoiding wind turbines, generic solar farms, and ESG-style stock imagery).

### Content Direction
- Commercial outcomes before technical detail.
- Remove duplication.
- Eliminate Greenly placeholder content.
- Replace demo copy with approved client content.
- Focus on measurable business outcomes.
- Plain UK English.
- Executive-first communication.

### UX
- Calm.
- Professional.
- Easy to scan.
- Executive.
- Minimal interaction.
- High readability.

### Scope Decisions
- CMS will be implemented after Sprint 2 approval.
- ACF Pro recommended before CMS implementation.
- Homepage redesign precedes CMS implementation.
- Repository-driven development adopted internally.

The final item — repository-driven development — is an **internal delivery methodology** adopted by the delivery team, not a client requirement. It is recorded here for completeness and traceability only, and shall not be treated as client-approved direction on par with the decisions above it.
